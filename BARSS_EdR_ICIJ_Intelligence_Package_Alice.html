1. Executive Summary

This report cross-references the intelligence packages provided by Alice Bouladon (March 1–6, 2026) against the ICIJ Offshore Leaks database (626 MB, 494K entities, 929K officers, 3.5M relationships) and the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals list (5.4 MB, ~12K entries).

The automated entity resolution produced 13 positive actor identifications across Panama Papers, Paradise Papers, Pandora Papers, Bahamas Leaks, and the original Offshore Leaks. Twenty-four actors returned negative results. The findings transform several of Alice's intelligence threads from reported allegations into independently verifiable public-record data.

Three Headline Findings

1. EdR intermediated 253+ shell entities across four ICIJ leaks. This is not a bank that occasionally facilitated offshore structures. This is industrial-scale shell production, with the Bahamas subsidiary alone intermediating 147 entities.

2. CEO Marc Ambroisien had his own personal bearer-share shell (Luciole Strategies, Seychelles) intermediated by his own bank — the identical pattern used for the Hansen Invest vehicles in the Bogdanchikov fraud.

3. One Panamanian law firm (Alcogal) connects seven actors across three continents: Haiti's BAM BAM oligarchs, sanctioned Russian oligarchs (Rotenberg), and both EdR Luxembourg fraud victim families (Bogdanchikov and Kaplun).

12.2 Legal Implications

A. Pattern of Practice (Not Isolated Incidents)

EdR's role as intermediary for bearer-share shells in the Bogdanchikov fraud, combined with the CEO's own use of the same structures, combined with the 1MDB conviction, establishes a pattern of institutional conduct. Any future litigation can argue systemic culture rather than rogue employees. The three scandals used the same institutional infrastructure.

B. Sanctions Compliance Question

Thewes managed Rotenberg accounts from 1999 to 2016. The Rotenbergs were sanctioned in March 2014 (EO 13661). This raises the question: did EdR Luxembourg properly exit or freeze Rotenberg-related accounts after March 2014? If not, this is a separate sanctions violation exposure beyond the 1MDB and Bogdanchikov matters.

C. The Alcogal Referral Question

If EdR was referring clients to Alcogal (provable through client files or correspondence), the bank's role extends beyond intermediating shells at Mossack Fonseca to actively directing clients toward specific offshore service providers. This would strengthen claims of institutional complicity in asset concealment.

D. Ruemmler's Statement Vulnerability

Her CNN statement (December 2025): "I knew Jeffrey Epstein in a professional capacity... he was a business referral source. I did not represent him and was not compensated by him." The EFTA emails show: spa trips paid by Epstein (EFTA02196386), furniture shipped to Epstein's island (EFTA01732271), and real-time DOJ penalty intelligence shared with Epstein (EFTA02499978). "Not compensated by him" may be technically true (Latham billed EdR, not Epstein) but the relationship was clearly not limited to "business referral."

CSSF Administrative Measure — Full Text (March 27, 2020)

MESURE ADMINISTRATIVE

Décision constatant la perte de l'honorabilité professionnelle de Monsieur Marc AMBROISIEN

La CSSF a décidé que Monsieur Marc AMBROISIEN ne remplit plus les exigences d'honorabilité professionnelle pour une durée de 10 ans.

Cette décision a été prise en raison de manquements graves que la CSSF a constatés individuellement et personnellement dans le chef de Monsieur Marc AMBROISIEN en lien avec l'exercice des fonctions d'ancien directeur agréé et d'ancien membre du conseil d'administration que Monsieur Marc AMBROISIEN a occupées auprès d'un établissement de crédit au Luxembourg, et notamment :

• Pour avoir manqué à ses obligations de veiller à l'obligation pour cet établissement de crédit de mettre en place un solide dispositif de gouvernance interne et une gestion saine et prudente des risques ;
